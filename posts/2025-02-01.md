My drawings have been pretty bad lately. I learned to compose accurately, and then gradually
                added abstraction. I would think of an process or object, and it would gradually arise out of drawing
                unrelated things. Now my choices are abstraction, realism, or landscapes, none of which are what I want
                to do. Charcoal helps a good deal. But it's still an issue with the medium.

Here is a very true statement, nevertheless in which room for interpretation is great:
                <i>History is the production and reproduction of real life</i>. What you call <i>real life</i> is not
                what I would, but what history promises, in one way or another, is the ability to see how others felt.
                What <i>production</i> and <i>reproduction</i> reflect could be artificial and natural processes, life
                and art etc. Perhaps it is the sense of situation which reproduction replicates, while production fails
                to capture. Maybe <i>production</i> is doing too much work, but can't you imagine what it means? An
                orchestra, a factory, a game of chess. What is articulated in the statement is the expressive potential
                of history, an ambition which stretches your hands behind your head and tries to touch the ground.

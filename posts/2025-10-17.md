(10.17.2025) <i>As Eve Said to the Serpent: On Landscape, Gender, and Art, Scapeland, Rebecca Solnit </i>
                <br><br>
                Our aesthetic language has been degenererating over the past century or so.... the picturesque was the
                territory of the rough, the irregular, and the idiosyncratic. The sublime was the territory of the
                phenomena that inspired terror, awe, and a sense of overwhelming majesty. The beauitful was specifically
                characterized as the pleasing, the harmonious, the smooth. "The passion caused by the great and the
                sublime in nature", writes Edmund Burke, "is astonishment, and astonishment is the state of the soul, in
                which all its motions are supsended, with some degree of horror".

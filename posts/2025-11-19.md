(11.19.2025) <i>Shroud</i> by Adrian Tchaikovsky is really good! Reminds me of the hawk who hunts with
                traffic lights.

(11.19.2025) <s>As I grow more confident with my writing and read more math my impulse to imprint on my
                    surroundings, whether through annotation or illustrated methods, has reduced.</s>
                I make less, read more. But there isn't anything wrong with this. What I am scared of is my bar for
                creative output rising so high that I suffocate and die. It's better to keep trying to grow in different
                ways.

(11.19.2025) My english class wants me to use everything. I don't want to use everything. It verges on
                practiced ignorance yet I need to believe in the irreducibility of gazing across chain-linked trees, the
                yellow leaves flickering only here and now. <br><br>
                Rebecca Solnit agrees: the emotion stirred by the landscape is piercing, a joy close to pain when the
                blue is deepest on the horizon or the clouds are doing those spectacular fleeting things so much easier
                to recall than to describe. If there is a tightness to her writing it is actively practiced, no one
                could say the beauty is in the words themselves.

Ray Bradbury, <i>Fahrenheit 451</i>:

'Whirl man's mind around so fast under the pumping hand of publishers, exploiters, broadcasters that the centrifuge flings off all unnecessary, time-wasting thought!'...<br><br>

'The zipper dipslaces the button and a man lacks just that much time to think while dressing at dawn, a philosophical hour, and thus a melancholy hour.'.<br><br>

'Cram them full of noncombustible data, chock them so damned full of 'facts' they feel stuffed, but absolutely 'brilliant' with information. Then they'll feel they're thinking, they'll get a sense of motion without moving. And they'll be happy, because facts of that sort don't change'<br><br>

His hands stayed on his knees, numbed and useless. "I don't talk <i>things</i>, sir". said Faber. "I talk the <i>meanings</i> of things. I sit here and <i> know </i> I'm alive."

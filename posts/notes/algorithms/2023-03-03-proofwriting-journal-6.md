    how did this weeks problem set go? 
        which problems did you find challenging?
        which problems did you find stressful?
        which problems did you find fun?
    how have your previous problem sets felt? 
    do you find yourself spending more time solving problems or writing up your solutions?
    do you feel you're approaching your problem sets any differently now than you might have before taking this course? If so, how so?


This week's problem set was the first significantly challenging one for me and I enjoyed it a lot. I found problems #4 and #5 challenging, particularly the subtractive/additive one and the n + 2 choose 3 one. Neither felt especially stressful: I did struggle with doubts about #3 for some time until the problem statement was clarified. 

The really fun problems included the subtractive/additive one, because it involved combining several substantial parts of counting and probability and the use of my own intuition, and upon reflection, problem #3. I also enjoyed the second proof from problem 4, which had both macro (which set to choose from?) and micro (how many ways to choose from the set?) levels.

This time, I found myself spending way more time solving problems than writing solutions, although this was the first homework from proofwriting where this was the case. For example, the logic tables in Problem Set 3 was good for practicing LaTex but very tedious to create.

I feel like I am approaching my problems in a more clear and separable way than I did before. Counting problems in particular require this approach (Either X, or Y) and I found myself rewording my solutions even as I progressed within the problem set.

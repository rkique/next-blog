##### Midterm review

**When to use distributed systems?**
- When the recipients are geo-distributed across the system (e.g. Google)
- Very large number of users / clients.
- Avoid a single point of failure
- Lots of objects/data to store
- When it is more cost-effective to scale across nodes than vertically.

**When not to use distributed systems?**
- When load can be handled by a single computer
- Security concerns, privacy concerns
- Consistency, Availabiity, Partition Impossibility result.
	- Cannot have a consistently available  

**Scenario: Shopping Cart for East and West**

> Fiber cut happens, East and West cannot communicate. 
> With a request, must choose either
>  **Consistency + Partition Tolerance.**
> 	Cart temporarily unavailable
> 	This allows the state shown to always be consistent.
> **Availability + Partition Tolerance**
> 	Accepts add and remove operations.
> 	But, consistency could be violated.
> 	Could be true for eventual consistency, or for conflict resolution.

newer conceptions of the lexicon, not as a list but as a network with tight interconnections, provide the insight that listing two related words, such as start, started, does not take up as much cognitive space as listing two unrelated words, such as start, flower (Bybee, 1985). Thus connectionist models (Rumelhart & McClelland, 1986) and analogical models (Eddington, 2000; Skousen, 1989, 1992) have storage of whole words with morphological relations emergent from the categorization involved in storage.

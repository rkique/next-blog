#### David Lightfoot Natural Selection (2012)


- Physical laws: limits to evolutionary change
- Singularists: just one factor, which is natural selection for shaping evolution

- the mental machinery for reading and writing originated as an exaptation which was co-opted later.
- The result of natural selection is adaptation: shaping of form, function, and behavior.

The primary question posed by the article is how we might connect the subjacency condition within UG with biological imperatives in natural selection. The immediate similarities I noticed were between the notion of exaptation and cultural transmission. Lightfoot mentions that reading and writing are likely to be exaptations, with no biological constraints present. However, any groups who discovered any capability for reading and writing could survive and dominate other groups; at the level of cultural transmission, such skills would be valuable and/or viral. 
Similarly, the existence of specific conditions within universal grammar don't need to be intrinsically tied to individual chances for reproduction. Instead, for them to be valuable they only need to be expressive, improving the ability of an individual to say exactly what it is they mean. Petty naively, I would hypothesize that *"What did you hear a rumor that she bought?" can be broken into two shorter and holistically more comprehensible expressions, while "What did you hear she bought?" is not breakable.

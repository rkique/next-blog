- non-standard orthography dramatizes power and status differentials between language varieties and their speakers.
- Orthography is a tool of `Abstandsprache`: language by difference. 

- ==Creation of the 'Mother Tongue': self and community united in a shared primordial attachment to a language==

- Once people step out of the framework of standard orthographies with categorical authority, they often manifest a desire to see their linguistic identities recognized by that orthography, to have an *intimate relationship* with the form of the *written word*.

- They retrieve the expectations about the relationship between form and meaning that children bring to their early spelling: the overriding function of spelling becomes indexical: assigning relations between words and their meanigns.

- It has the potential, as I have mentioned above, to reinvest the generic and written with the diversity of spoken language.

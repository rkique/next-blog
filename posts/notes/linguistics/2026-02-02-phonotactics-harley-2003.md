Phonotactic rule 1: All phonological words contain at least one syllable, and one vowel

People pay attention to syllables in spoken speech. Linebreaking words in a bounding box is useful.

Phonotactic rule 2: Sequences of repeated consonants are not possible.


Rule 3: ng never occurs in the onset of a syllable. Onset is the consonants at the beginning of the syllable.

(C)(C)(C)V(C)(C)(C)(C)

Rule 4: /h/ is never found at the end of a syllable (coda).

Rule 5: The affricates /tS/ and /dZ/ and the glottal fricative /h/ cannot appear in complex onsets. There are no words like "chlink", "jlam", "khot". 

Sonority: obstruents/occlusives,fricatives, nasals, liquids, vowels. 

Rule 6: The first consonant in a two-consonant onset must be an obstruent. Sequences like trʌk, drɑp, we never have liquid in the beginning followed by obstruent such as rtʌk or rdɑp. This agrees with the Sonority Sequencing Principle: obstruents are lower sonority than the second factor.

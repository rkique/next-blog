Questioning link between symbolic material culture and syntactic language. Without syntax in language, it would not have been possible to convey across individuals or across groups, the meanings of material symbols. 

A high level of technical competence is an argument for language?
- H. Sapiens were behaviorally "modern" before they left Africa

The ability to store and apply symbols externally: material culture intervening directly on social behavior. 

Blombos Cave from c. 77-73 ky. include M1 and M2 phases. M1 phase include bifacial points, bone tools, marine shell beads, and engraved ochre. Beads are indicative of modern human behavior. necklaces, bracelets. A shared behavior with symbolic meaning within the group. 

What cognitive abilities are required to form symbolic references in beads and other artifacts. Symbolic reference itself is not difficult. The wearing of beads suggests that one person can understand how she looks from the perspective of another. This ability to understand the intentions of others is a key to language. 

> The child has to explain that someone thinks an object is somewhere, even if the child knows the object is somewhere else.

> Symbolic reference: should be used in language.

iconic relationships between objects and their represetnations. Pretend play becomes more complex and creative with time and can be said to be symbolic in a weak sense as it involves treating an object as if it were something else.

Apes do understand intentions, but used in competitive rather than cooperative contexts. As young as 15 months children expect people to behave according to their own false beliefs, not with true ones.

How to understand beads as being either symbolic or aesthetic/decorative?

Style as an indicator of modernity, even without conveying symbolic meaning. Children who lack complete ToM and level-2 perspective taking are incapable of holding in mind a basic and a subordinate category. 

Harpoons fall into various subordinate categories: *barbed bone points, bone points, bone tools*. 

d'Errico 's view on recursive syntax is that it is necessary to articulate conflicting perspectives. It is the only means of communication, bearing a built-in meta-language that permits creation and transmission of other symbolic codes. 

If modern cognition precedes language, then syntactic recursion has its own computational basis. 

The authors of the Blombos paper claim that the capability to represent how an object appears to another person (level-2 perspective-taking) enables the invention of symbolic artifacts, and moreover constitutes the ability to perform recursion, which enables syntactic language. As noted by the authors, this argument relies on an epistemological question, that of the beads being truly symbolic as opposed to decorative. While it indeed seems difficult to justify the "semioticity" of any archeological artifact, I wonder if we could, for instance, look at the earliest records of written language, which is more readily analyzable as syntactic. Then, arguments from this later time period could perhaps apply to the artifacts here.

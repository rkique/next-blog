##### The Construction Grammar view of language

A language is akin to a species. A linguistic "genotype" corresponds to the neural representation of an idiolect, instantiated by a collection of mental representations. The fitness of individual constructions is intertwined with those of other constructions: constructions are a part of a (linguistic) system.

##### Usage

Bybee (2007) has shown how frequency of occurrence plays an important role in shaping language from phonology to morphology to morphosyntax, due to the effects of repeated processing experiences with specific examples. Additionally, groups of constructions overlapping in syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic properties emerge and form the basis for usage-based generalizations.

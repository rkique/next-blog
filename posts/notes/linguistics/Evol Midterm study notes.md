**Critiques of Hockett's list**
- modality flexibility: 
	- Actually, linguistic systems can be used across modalities. No need for the vocal-auditory channel or the notion of rapid fading. Brain acquires and processes languages similarly regardless of modality.
	- Linguistic expression involves many *semiotic strategies*
		- Iconic use of words
		- prosody
		- co-speech gestures
- Ostensive communication
	- Making it clear to someone we are intending to communicate something. Receiver must make inferences to understand what is being communicated.
	- communicates that we are intentionally communicating – and sometimes communicating things beyond the message alone

- Social signaling
	- Language as social marker: language as a cue for social group membership. Could be a positive adaptation for language (niche construction: organisms impacting environment shape local selection).

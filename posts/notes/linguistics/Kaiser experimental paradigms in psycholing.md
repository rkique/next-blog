There is only a small region of the retina, the fovea, which allows for high-acuity vision. There are two kinds of reading, fixations, and saccades, which are rapid eye movement. They last 20-50 milliseconds, move over six to nine characters. Fixation is interpreted as a period of time when information is being obtained. 10-15 percent are regressions. Fixations last longer than saccades, 200-250 ms on average. Less skilled readers have shorter saccades, longer fixations, and more regressions.

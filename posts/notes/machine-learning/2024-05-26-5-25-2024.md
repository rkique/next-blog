Mistborn Era 2 review

What I really like about Brandon Sanderson is how consistent he is at presenting his ideas, again and again and again until you start to feel their weight. In a certain sense this is a talent for world-building, but in my opinion his worlds are not super lucidly presented or particularly beautiful in their own right, there are plenty of more vividly imaginative and less compelling writers out there. 

His characters are almost boringly realistic individuals, and you get to encounter them again and again from different internal monologues that nearly inevitably have the same message. Redundancy is employed here really well. Compared to other novels at times its comparing a japanese ink painting to paint splatters. There isn't a real plot arc over the course of these particular novels but instead a series of plot points at a very rapid pace. 

The things that I have been reminded of. The most powerful pulling forces on me have always been fantastical ones that dare me to achieve impossible things.

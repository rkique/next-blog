### perplexity
The inverse geometric mean of an unseen set of test words:

$\sqrt[N]{\frac{1}{P(w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_n)}}$

Perplexity-plagiarism tradeoff

Using N-Grams for modeling long-tail distributions is ... not a good idea.
- common words will be overrepresented and rare words will be nonexistent

Sparseness is the big problem with N-grams

Laplace smoothing:
- add 1 to (bigram) matrix

Kneser-Ney smoothing

Counting frequencies stores SEMANTIC and SYNTACTIC and DISCOURSE information...

Linear interpolation:
- Weighted combination of unigram/bigram/trigram

Sonic Safe offers a one-stop, preventative, and water-conscious property protection solution that uses low-frequency sound waves to stop the spread of wildfires.

The reaction time between wildfires and firefighter discharge is unreliable, and areas prone to critical wildfires risk water and staff shortages. The 24-day Palisades wildfire in Los Angeles revealed a shortage of firefighters unable to battle the wind speeds. Four times the normal water demand was used for over 15 hours straight—some even creating makeshift water sprinklers—depleting fire hydrants and reservoirs and making current water-based solutions ineffective. California residents we spoke to primarily rely on insurance and cited worries about the lack of outdoor fire protection systems.

By disrupting the air-oxygen barrier, our proprietary system extinguishes fires and prevents ember ignition without water or safety risks. Installed by square footage, it surrounds properties with interconnected speakers activated using fire-detection software. Compared to traditional protection strategies, Sonic Safe does not require a water supply and is more reliable in preventing destruction.

Sonic Safe is well-positioned to prevent $150B of annual property damages in an unsaturated, low-competition market. The US market for wildfire protection systems totaled $8.5 billion in 2023 with a 12.5% CAGR. Property damage from wildfires costs U.S. businesses and homeowners $150 billion annually. Due to climate change and unmanaged development, wildfires have been more frequent and devastating. One University of Colorado Boulder investigation found that wildfires in the Western U.S. destroyed 246% more homes and structures between the past two decades. Sonic Safe strives to mitigate this trend.

Our upfront payment system and subscription-based model will be highly profitable following the establishment of our solution’s efficacy. Sonic Safe installations are paid upfront, with pricing based on square footage. Maintenance and upgrade fees are charged on a recurring basis. Beginning with government contracts to build credibility, we will pivot toward a DTC model targeting homeowners. After securing government contracts, residents will be acquired through insurance partnerships and digital ads. Over time, increasing media exposure and buy-in will allow us to establish a monopoly on sonic technology as a cost-effective alternative to water-based systems.

Our team brings expertise in relevant sectors. With a strong research foundation at the Brown School of Engineering and guidance from professors who specialize in combustion processes, we are well-equipped for prototype research and development. Our expertise in public health and economics suggests we will be financially responsible in managing the research process.

  

By year five, we expect over $435 million in net sales operating within the continental U.S. Our LTV/CAC ratio, between 2.8-18, assumes a 3:2 mix between residential and commercial customers, with commercial clients offering higher revenue and lower overhead. We are raising $1.5 million in initial investment to propel R&D efforts in the first year and build confidence in our product. This will fund research in the first year and secure test trials with organizations invested in wildfire prevention or fire extinguishing technology; comparable products have undergone testing on manufacturing sites for the U.S. Air Force and the Department of Defense.

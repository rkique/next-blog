When I was very young I had the conscious realization that every conversation I participated in could be altered drastically by complete incomprehension on my part. I could say "Soup" again and again, or repeat back the last two words, or make a high pitched humming noise...

In books like the Phantom Tollbooth the characters misinterpret patterns of speech halfways. When faced with a problem, they reference a problem that seems smaller but isn't really.

Here, the patterns of interpretation of the characters are very clear. When faced with a problem, they make excuses that don't seem like excuses. They are smart, reasonable beings that happen to always point to something that points back to themselves.

Socrates believes that those who seek bad things are simply misguided; that is, they do not know those things are bad. He uses Meno's notion of a bad thing -- such as greed -- to illustrate that although someone may believe a thing to be bad, they still believe it benefits them. 

I read this as elevating the human will above other considerations: although someone might commit suicide knowing it is bad relative to the judgements of society, Socrates implies that their internal calculus makes suicide good, or at least more appealing than the alternative.

Meno is saying that he himself can't believe it... but who's to trust Meno with basic opinion?

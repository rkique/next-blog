title: 'error_theory_sum'
---
A common argument is that the error theory states that there are no normative properties. Thus, the error theory entails that there is no reason to believe the error theory, as the property of being a reason for belief is a normative theory.

This is not the argument Streumer makes. Streumer argues that the above only shows that if the error theory is true, there is no reason to believe the error theory. But we do not believe the error theory.

It is not a problem for a theory if we cannot believe it; it could still be true. Our inability to sincerely say that a theorem is true does nothing to show it is false.

title: 'indexical'
---
we may be able to view moral judgements as indexicals: a natural property independent of other attributes of time, location etc.

We cannot deduce indexical judgements from non-indexical ones. For example, unless we already know which day is today (more naturally, unless we know what day it is, an indexical kind of knowledge), we cannot deduce from the date ofan event whether it occurred yesterday. Similarly, the moral properties of an event do not follow analytically from its natural properties.

### in what ways are moral judgements NOT indexical?

There's a sense in which they have permanence, whereas indexicals don't have permanence. But the *meaning* of "here" is usually clear, as is the *meaning* of "good". Can we draw more parallels between them?

- At least with "here" and "now", both can be imperatives: "come here", "be good". 

- here has the property of "to-be-locatedness" and now has the property of "to-be-passedness"

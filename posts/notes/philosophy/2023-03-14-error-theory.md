### OBJECTIONS TO 1
Error theory says all moral judgements are false, because of Mackie's queerness argument or something
at a stretch, we may be able to say that error theory says,
    - "we have no reason to believe in metaphysical properties"
    - However, believing in metaphysical properties are not the same as believing in "theory".

There are differences between reasons for belief (metareasons, rules for belief) and reasons for action. (reason, rules for doing) That you ought to believe does depend on what else you believe. What you morally ought to do doesn't depend on what you happen to desire. Thus, the error theory does not entail that we have no reasons to believe things.

# streumer's argument (SILLY!)
(1) the error theory entails that """we have no reasons to believe things.""" (lol)

(2) we cannot fail to believe what we believe to be entailed by a small number of our own beliefs (such as the error theory)

(3) we cannot have a belief while believing that there is no reason to have this belief.

Thus, we cannot beleive things.

- The reasons to think that morality is a myth are grounded in the problem with categorical reasons (queerness)
- But epistemology is all about categorical reasons. (abstract entailments)
- The same reasons lead us to the conclusion that reasons-to-believe are mythical.
- However this undermines the basis of our reasoning.

belief aims at the truth: if you believe these things, you can add beliefs to your belief-stock.

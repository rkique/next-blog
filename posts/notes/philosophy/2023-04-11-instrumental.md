Some things are desirable because of what they will bring -- that is, they are instrumental.
- Money
- Living on the East Coast
- A high-speed internet connection

Other things are desirable in-and-of-themselves: that is, there is something implicit to them that is important

- Happiness
- Respect of colleagues
- Preventing suffering

Wanting de dicto and de re
is it: Mikayla wants to meet the world's best cellist.

- de re: x is the world's best cellist, Mikayla wants to meet x
- de dicto: Mikayla wants: (x is the world's best cellist and Mikayla meets x). This is more superficial.

- The direct path to happiness

Example of why de re makes more sense than de dicto: Tom Sawyer decided to do what was right de re by (emotional ?)motivation, but his conception of what was wrong was entirely de dicto. Maybe this bridges the gap between natural and moral properties?

Maybe the law is meant to be a way of enforcing rational behavior that is also good (?). Maybe if you follow the law de dicto you are doing the right thing, most of the time.

Good for a person: we can look at their subjective interests. Where we are authoritative, but not omniscient. Once you imagine what it is like...

Subjective interests: the things which you believe are good for you. 

Objective interests: the things that are actually good for you.

## Understanding Embeddings GISP

This group independent study course will review recent and ongoing work in vector embeddings (focusing primarily on language, but depending on interests and available material also images, music, and commercial/social applications). It will focus on interpretability, theoretical implications and limitations, and further research directions.

If the course had to pose a question, it would be:

"How can embeddings help us understand ourselves and others better? In what ways can we capture implicit understanding?"

This course is unique because (1): it will focus on embeddings alone, and (2): as much as possible, it will have both technical and theoretical depth. There are a few courses that cover similar content, such as Language Processing in Humans and Machines, or Computational Linguistics. However, unlike most discussion-based courses, students will perform experiments and attempt to implement results. Unlike CSCI 1460, this course focuses primarily on embeddings and their theoretical grounding.

The course may involve reimplementing key papers and results.

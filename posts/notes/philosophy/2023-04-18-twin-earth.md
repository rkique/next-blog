Meaning ain't in the head. 
The reference of natural kind terms are not determined completely by what is inside the speaker.

If the hypothesis that moral properties are derived from natural properties is true, then imagine a moral twin earth exactly like our own, except where everyone is a consequentialist. Now imagine an otherwise perfectly healthy appendectomy patient and five people each missing a vital organ. The consequentialists want to sacrifice them; we would not.

The hypothesis would say that we are not really in disagreement, because we have exactly the same natural environment. But clearly, we are. Thus, moral properties cannot be contingent on natural ones.

There is the argument that this metaphysical experiment is metaethically insignificant. Because the difference between the way Twin Earthers speak to one another is empirically going to be different than our own, even if our intuitions tell us otherwise.

Also, for differences in our moral environment to exist, then there must be some natural difference as well, right?

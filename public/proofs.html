Space Logic Lemmas

Here I will introduce some basic proof structures in an intuitive format, namely that of circles. It's borrowing a lot and perhaps scratching the surface of theories With Names, but I think these results on their own are very fun, self-contained, and intuitive.

Everything*

A

A and B

The first example here is set intersection. Given sets A and B, their intersection is all of the elements in both set A and in set B. This is the overlapping area in a world between A and B.

A implies B

With the use of "implies", we have moved out of statements in worlds to statements about worlds. We are not looking at areas within a world. We are talking about comparing areas across worlds. In every world with A, we have B.

Not A

Not B

not B implies not A

This is the intuitive explanation for the contrapositive. Not B encompasses Not A because A encompasses B. This is like downward entailment in semantics, and the basis for the mathematical contrapositive proof method.

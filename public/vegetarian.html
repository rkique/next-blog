How to go vegetarian

Step 1. Cook for yourself.

That's it!

List of things that are easy on vegetarian diet:

  • Everything is edible. Even if you undercook something it will still be edible and not taste too bad.
  • Abundance of low/ no-cook foods. Tofu, scrambled eggs, peanut butter, stirfried vegetables, potatoes, pasta, rice, peanut butter toast, trail mix, protein drinks, fruits, beans, corn, salad, hashbrowns, tea/coffee/milk, premade soup, peanut butter pretzels etc.
  • Cost. These are not traditionally considered healthy items and do not get markups.
  • Clean up

List of things that are difficult on non-vegetarian diet:

  • Food safety: having a dedicated knife for red meat, a cutting board, and managing shelf life.
  • Knowledge of recipes. Just looking at vegetarian dishes there are still so many recipes I will never be able to try.
  • Transporting uncooked food from store to apartment.
  • Food poisoning risks

I also eat frozen shrimp and salmon, beyond meat items, and normal dishes at restaurants. I don't really think of myself as a vegetarian, but I agree that consuming less meat is one of the most impactful choices you can make as a consumer.
